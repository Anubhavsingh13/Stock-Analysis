## Executive Summary
3B BlackBio DX makes branded real-time PCR / molecular-diagnostic kits (TRUPCR) plus, since Jul-2025, rapid lateral-flow kits via its Belgian acquisition Coris BioConcept — a capital-light, debt-free, high-ROCE (25.5%) IP/consumables business demerged out of the erstwhile Kilpest India. **The thesis tension:** headline FY26 revenue +47% (₹142 Cr) and PAT +26% (₹60 Cr) look excellent, but ~₹36 Cr of that revenue is the inorganic Coris consolidation and the *core standalone* diagnostics business has stalled to **2–3% organic growth**, losing share in a market growing ~17% CAGR to better-funded domestic rivals (Molbio, Mylab) and Chinese price competition. **Key risk:** commoditisation of PCR kits + capital sitting idle in a ₹158 Cr treasury (which supplies ~⅓ of profit) rather than compounding in the business. Stock is in a Weinstein **Stage 4 downtrend**, ~42% below its 52-week high, offering no margin of safety at a treasury-adjusted core P/E of ~21–25×.

## BUSINESS PRIMER

**What it does / how it makes money.** 3B BlackBio DX is a Bhopal-based molecular-diagnostics company that designs, manufactures and sells *in-vitro diagnostic (IVD) test kits* — principally real-time PCR ("qPCR") assays, NGS panels, nucleic-acid extraction kits and (via Coris) rapid antigen/lateral-flow tests — under the **TRUPCR®** brand. Customers are B2B: hospital and reference diagnostic laboratories in India and, increasingly, distributors across Europe, the Middle East, Africa and Latin America (channelled through UK subsidiary TRUPCR Europe Ltd and now Coris in Belgium). Revenue is essentially *kits × price* — a consumables/reagent model: labs run 3B's open-system kits on their own thermocyclers, generating recurring reagent pull-through. Therapeutic menu spans oncology/haematology (leukaemia, BCR-ABL, solid-tumour markers), infectious disease (TB, HIV, HCV, STIs), antimicrobial resistance (AMR) and human genetics. A small, declining agrochemical export line (a Kilpest legacy) remains. The business absolutely depends on two things: (1) a broad, regulatorily-approved (CE-IVD / CDSCO / soon US-FDA) assay menu, and (2) lab relationships/brand that keep those labs buying 3B kits over cheaper Chinese/MNC alternatives.

**What kind of business / what to watch.** This is a **capital-light, branded-IP / specialty-consumables business** — debt-free, ROCE ~25%, tiny fixed-asset base (₹28 Cr gross block against ₹142 Cr revenue), and a very large treasury (₹158 Cr of investments, ~15% of market cap) that throws off ₹20–26 Cr of "other income" and materially distorts reported earnings. The key value drivers are *menu breadth, regulatory approvals, and export expansion*; the key risks are *technology/price commoditisation* (Chinese PCR-kit pricing, MNC competition), *loss of the post-COVID demand halo*, and *capital-allocation drift* (idle treasury, 0.33% dividend yield). Two structural features colour the entire analysis: (a) a one-time COVID RT-PCR super-spike in FY21 (revenue ₹227 Cr / PAT ₹114 Cr) that has fully normalised and must NOT be extrapolated; and (b) the treasury portfolio that means reported PAT overstates operating earnings power by roughly a third.
