## Business Primer

Oswal Pumps Limited, incorporated in 2003 and headquartered in Karnal, Haryana, manufactures solar-powered submersible pumps, grid-connected pumps, electric motors, and solar modules. Revenue flows primarily from two streams: (1) solar pump supply and turnkey installation under PM-KUSUM Component B and state-level agricultural solar pump schemes (~80% of revenue), where the company supplies complete systems — pump, solar module, mounting structure, controller, and installation — to state nodal agencies; and (2) conventional grid-connected pump sales through a dealer network and exports (~20%). Listed June 2025 at ₹614/share; IPO oversubscribed 34×. The singular thing the business absolutely depends on: uninterrupted PM-KUSUM scheme disbursements + government receivable collections.

This is a vertically integrating capital-goods manufacturer at an earlier scale stage than Shakti Pumps, with a significantly more ambitious diversification agenda (rooftop solar, C&I, utility, inverters). FY26 revenue ₹2,064 Cr (+44% YoY) and PAT margin 18% look exceptional. The structural feature dominating the analysis: cash conversion cycle of 218 days with trade receivables ₹1,128 Cr (55% of revenue) — structural government collection behavior. Simultaneously: IPO proceeds 30% unspent, module capacity expanding 600 MW → 2.1 GW (no cell manufacturing), and pivoting into ₹1,000 Cr non-pump revenue target for FY27.
