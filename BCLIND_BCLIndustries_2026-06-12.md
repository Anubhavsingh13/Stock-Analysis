BCL Industries is India's largest grain-based distillery in North India, undergoing a deliberate transformation from a low-margin edible oil refiner to a high-margin ethanol+ENA+IMIL producer. The stock trades at ~9× trailing P/E — the steepest discount in its peer group (which ranges 13–41×) — despite FY26 PAT growing 77% YoY to ₹126 Cr. The PIE model implies the market is pricing negative NOPAT growth against a company expanding capacity 40-50% over the next 2 years. Expected value: ₹96.5 per share (+151% from ₹38.4). Key risks: OMC ethanol policy volatility and maize price spikes. Buy in tranches at current levels; add on confirmed Stage 2 breakout above ₹48.
