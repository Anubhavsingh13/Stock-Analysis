## BUSINESS PRIMER

### What the business does
Waaree Energies Limited is India's largest solar photovoltaic module manufacturer and, as of FY26, the largest non-Chinese solar module manufacturer globally by installed capacity (22.77 GW worldwide: 20.17 GW in India + 2.6 GW in Texas, USA). The company designs, manufactures, and sells solar modules — primarily TOPCon and mono-PERC technology — to utility-scale solar project developers, EPC contractors, rooftop installers, and government agencies in India and internationally. Revenue flows through a simple model: volume (GW shipped) × realization (₹/watt). In FY26, 12.6 GW of modules were shipped (highest ever), generating ₹26,537 Cr revenue at an implied ASP of ~₹21.1/W. The outstanding order book of ₹53,000 Cr (65–70% exports/overseas) provides 2× annual revenue visibility. Two policy pillars underpin the business: (1) India's Basic Customs Duty (BCD of 40% on modules, 25% on cells) protecting domestic pricing from Chinese undercutting, and (2) US Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) domestic manufacturing incentives that make Texas-manufactured modules uniquely competitive in the US market, where Indian imports face 126%+ effective tariffs.

### What kind of business this is
Waaree Energies is mid-transition: from a capital-light module assembler (FY20–FY23) to a fully integrated, capital-intensive solar manufacturer — "Waaree 2.0." The ₹30,000 Cr capex programme (glass: ₹3,900 Cr; ingot & wafer: ₹6,200 Cr in Nagpur; BESS; inverters; green hydrogen; Oman polysilicon stake) is the defining investment thesis. Value creation depends on whether margins expand structurally as backward integration reduces input costs, or compress as commodity competition intensifies. The key forensic concern is working capital: despite PAT doubling to ₹3,884 Cr in FY26, OCF fell 49% to ₹1,627 Cr as the cash conversion cycle expanded from 35 to 90 days. This is the most important quality-of-earnings question at current valuations. The business is executing through a temporary cash trough; the strategic bet is that Waaree 2.0 emerges as a structurally lower-cost producer with a US manufacturing moat that no Chinese competitor can replicate.

## MODULE 1b — Sector Cycle

**Assessment: STRONG STRUCTURAL TAILWIND (Multi-year secular)**

**Secular drivers (>80% of demand):**
- India's 500 GW RE target by 2030 requires 150+ GW incremental solar from ~130 GW installed (June 2026). Implies 25–35 GW/year of solar additions for 4 years — every GW requires modules.
- PM Surya Ghar (rooftop solar for 10M homes), RPO mandates for DISCOMs, and industrial decarbonisation commitments are structural demand. Policy reversal risk is low — RE is bipartisan in India.
- US market: 30–35 GW/year utility solar demand; IRA incentives for domestic manufacturing; 126% effective tariff on imported modules. Waaree Texas is the only significant Indian-origin US domestic manufacturing operation. Strategic moat vs all non-US solar manufacturers.
- Global China+1: Europe (anti-dumping), Japan, Middle East, Southeast Asia all seeking non-Chinese solar supply chains. Waaree's brand credibility as a non-Chinese manufacturer is a differentiated selling point.

**Cyclical risk (~20%):**
- Chinese module spot ASP <USD 0.10/W (LONGi/Jinko dumping globally). India BCD creates a floor of ~USD 0.14/W landed cost for imports. If BCD is reduced/removed (unlikely given Make in India policy), domestic margins compress sharply.
- US tariff policy risk — any administration can modify Section 201/reciprocal tariffs. However, domestic manufacturing support is bipartisan (jobs, national security), reducing policy reversal risk.
- Global oversupply: PLI has triggered massive Indian capacity addition. If domestic demand grows slower than capacity, domestic price competition intensifies.

**Sector verdict: STRONG TAILWIND. Protected by BCD domestically; protected by IRA and 126% tariff in US. Main risk is policy reversal (low probability, high impact).**
