## Executive Summary

Astral Limited is India's leading CPVC pipe manufacturer and a growing multi-product chemicals and adhesives company, built over two decades by promoter-founder Sandeep Engineer. The company has a demonstrated track record of 15% revenue CAGR over a decade, near-zero debt, and consistently high ROCE (18–21%). The immediate investment thesis is anchored in a demerger announced June 25, 2026: the chemicals/adhesives/paints division (Astral Chemie, ₹1,266 crore FY26 revenue, ~21% of consolidated) will be separated as a standalone listed entity while the core plumbing business — which delivered a stunning Q4FY26 (volumes +24%, margins 22.9%) — remains in Astral Ltd. The key risk is the stretched valuation of the residual plumbing business (80x TTM P/E post-demerger announcement recovery) and the uncertain path to listing and re-rating for Astral Chemie, which faces Pidilite's 70%+ market dominance in adhesives and a not-yet-profitable paints venture.

## BUSINESS PRIMER

**What the business does and how it makes money:**
Astral Limited is a branded plastics and chemicals company headquartered in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, founded and led by CMD Sandeep Engineer since inception. The company earns the bulk of its revenue (71% in FY26, ₹4,679 crore) from its Pipes and Fittings division, which manufactures CPVC (chlorinated polyvinyl chloride), UPVC, HDPE, PEX, and other plastic pipes used in residential plumbing, drainage, irrigation, and industrial fluid handling. Revenue flows through a volume × realization model — 263,026 MT of pipe volumes sold in FY26 at an average realization of ~₹178/kg. The remaining 29% of revenue (₹1,890 crore in FY26) comes from the Adhesives, Sealants, and Construction Chemicals segment (branded as Astral Adhesives / Resinova, with a niche paints sub-segment from the 2022 Gem Paints acquisition now operated as Astral Coatings). Customers are a mix of institutional builders, contractors, and individual households through a 130,000+ dealer distribution network. Geographic concentration is strongly India-focused. The business absolutely depends on two things: (1) the health of India's residential construction and real estate market (drives CPVC plumbing penetration), and (2) Astral's ability to maintain pricing power and distribution reach against a growing field of organised competitors.

**What kind of business this is and what to watch:**
Astral is a branded consumer/industrial compounder — capital-light in relative terms (ROCE ~20%), building durable moats through brand, distribution, and technology. The key value driver is a combination of volume growth (driven by India's piped water penetration and real estate cycle), mix improvement (CPVC earns higher margins than UPVC/agriculture-grade PVC), and margin management around volatile raw material costs (PVC resin, CPVC compound). The key structural feature that colours the entire analysis is the just-announced demerger: effective June 2026, Astral is splitting itself into a pure-play plumbing company (the high-margin, high-quality crown jewel) and a separately listed chemicals/adhesives/paints entity (Astral Chemie — subscale vs. Pidilite, with an early-stage paints venture). Watch: (1) CPVC resin backward integration via Nexelon subsidiary — commissioning H2FY27, this is a 100–150 bps margin catalyst for the plumbing business; (2) Astral Chemie listing price and whether adhesives margins recover to 14–16% guided range; (3) whether volume growth momentum (15–16% annual vs. industry ~6–8%) is sustainable or was partly a restocking cycle post-FY25 destocking.

## MODULE 1b — Sector Cycle: Pipes & Building Products

### Secular vs. Cyclical Demand Decomposition

**Observed FY26 pipe volume growth for Astral: ~15.8% YoY (263,026 MT)**

**Decomposition:**

| Demand Component | Estimated Contribution | Durability |
|---|---|---|
| Secular — Residential construction + real estate cycle | ~7–8% | Durable; India needs 25–30 million housing units over next decade |
| Secular — Jal Jeevan Mission 2.0 (government piped water) | ~2–3% | Extended to 2028 with ₹4.8 billion additional budget; ~19% rural households (194 million) still lack tap water |
| Secular — CPVC penetration in plumbing (replacing GI/copper pipes) | ~2–3% | Structural; urban housing increasingly specifying CPVC for hot/cold plumbing |
| Cyclical — Channel restocking (FY25 had severe dealer destocking) | ~3–4% | Temporary; cycle reversal in FY26 inflated volumes above run-rate |
| **Structural Baseline (durable DCF anchor)** | **~11–12%** | This is the real demand growth rate once restocking fades |

**Warning:** Management's FY26 volume growth of 15.8% includes ~3–4 percentage points of channel restocking bounce-back from FY25's destocking environment. DCF should embed 11–13% pipe volume CAGR, not 15–16%.

**Input cost cycle:** PVC resin prices have been volatile but broadly range-bound in FY26. The CPVC compound (key input for Astral's specialty product) is being backward-integrated via Nexelon — reducing Astral's exposure to external pricing from H2FY27.

**Competitive capacity pipeline:** Hyderabad (70,000 MT, Astral, operational Oct 2024), Kanpur (~19,000 MT, Astral, started Oct 2025), and similar expansions across Supreme, Finolex, and regional players. The overall Indian plastic pipes industry is not in overcapacity — demand is outpacing supply at current growth rates — but simultaneous capacity additions could pressure industry utilization in FY28–FY29.

**Sector Cycle Verdict:** TAILWIND (structural) — The pipe sector sits in an enviable demand position: government spending (Jal Jeevan Mission 2.0 through 2028), real estate recovery, and structural CPVC penetration. The tailwind is secular, not purely cyclical. Duration: 3–5 years with high confidence. The cyclical restocking component will fade by Q1–Q2 FY27.

**Net FX Exposure:** Minimal — Astral is domestic-revenue focused. Raw materials have some USD exposure (CPVC resin imports), but backward integration via Nexelon progressively reduces this. No meaningful FX earnings sensitivity.
