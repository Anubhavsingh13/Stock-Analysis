## Executive Summary
Ganesha Ecosphere is India's largest PET-waste recycler and the most direct listed play on the EPR / mandatory-recycled-content regulatory tailwind (reaffirmed by MoEF's 31-Mar-2026 notification). But it is a **spread business, not a growth business** — FY26 proved it: revenue was flat (₹1,482cr vs ₹1,466cr) while PAT collapsed from ₹103cr to ₹38cr as the PET-waste-to-product spread compressed and new rPET capacity ramped at a loss. At ~80× trailing earnings, ~7.7% best-case ROIC (3.8% in FY26, both below WACC), ~32% promoter pledge on a low ~39% promoter stake, negative free cash flow every year since FY20, and a stock still ~29% below its 200-DMA, the regulatory story is real but the returns and the price are not yet aligned. Own the theme via the leader — but only once EBITDA/tonne recovery actually shows up and at a materially lower price.

## BUSINESS PRIMER
Ganesha Ecosphere collects used PET bottles (8+ billion / 150,000+ tonnes a year) and converts them into two product families. The **legacy business is RPSF** (Recycled Polyester Staple Fibre) + spun/dope-dyed yarn — a competitive, semi-commodity textile input at ~9–10% EBITDA margin, priced off virgin polyester. The **growth business is food-grade rPET** (bottle-to-bottle chips/granules), a higher-margin, harder-to-qualify product whose demand is being *created* by regulation (EPR mandates + minimum recycled-content rules). Revenue = volume (tonnes) × realization; profit = the **spread** between PET-waste input cost and finished-product price, times utilization. Plants are asset-heavy (Kanpur/Rudrapur legacy + Warangal rPET).

This is an **asset-heavy, mid-cycle industrial mid-transition** from commodity RPSF to regulation-driven rPET. The value driver is **spread × utilization** on new rPET lines; the risks are (1) input/realization volatility crushing the spread (exactly what hit FY26), (2) a heavy capex + rising-debt phase with chronically negative FCF, and (3) governance/financing signals — promoter holding only ~39% with ~32% pledged. Read the whole analysis as: a powerful multi-year regulatory tailwind vs. a currently sub-WACC, spread-cyclical, richly-valued business.
