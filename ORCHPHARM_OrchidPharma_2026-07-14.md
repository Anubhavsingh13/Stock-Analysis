## Executive Summary
Orchid Pharma is a **post-IBC turnaround** (Dhanuka Group acquired it out of bankruptcy via NCLT in 2020, wiping ~₹3,200cr of debt) whose ~₹5,370cr market cap is almost entirely a **call option on one novel antibiotic — Enmetazobactam / EXBLIFEP** (FDA-approved; management pegs lifetime potential at $1–2bn). Strip that optionality away and you have a low-return, cyclical cephalosporin API business that just had a bad year: FY26 revenue fell 12% to ₹811cr and standalone PAT collapsed to ₹45cr (consolidated just ₹20cr) as 7-ACA/antibiotic prices eroded and inventory was written down — and where **₹49cr of "other income" exceeded the entire ₹45cr core operating profit before depreciation.** At ~100–120× standalone earnings (higher on consolidated), sub-WACC ROIC, other-income-propped profits, a just-effective related-party merger with promoter entity Dhanuka Laboratories (161:5, effective 10-Jul-2026), and the stock near its 52-week high, this is a binary, venture-style bet — not a margin-of-safety investment. Exciting science; un-investable price for a 2–3 year value framework.

## BUSINESS PRIMER
Orchid Pharma develops and manufactures **cephalosporin-based active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) and finished dosage forms** — antibiotics — with backward integration into 7-ACA (7-aminocephalosporanic acid, the key cephalosporin intermediate). It sells APIs and formulations to pharma companies and distributors across regulated (US/Europe) and non-regulated (Africa, South Asia) markets; revenue is essentially volume × realization of antibiotic molecules, and profitability swings with the global antibiotic price cycle (heavily influenced by Chinese 7-ACA supply). The transformational asset is **Enmetazobactam (brand EXBLIFEP, a cefepime-enmetazobactam combination for resistant gram-negative infections)** — a *novel, patented* molecule Orchid invented; in FY26 it acquired 100% of Allecra Therapeutics (Germany/France) to own the molecule globally and is now commercializing it (India, Europe, and licensing deals for US, Russia, Japan, LatAm, SE Asia).

This is a **cyclical, asset-heavy commodity-API business carrying a high-value novel-drug option** — a barbell of low-quality base economics and one potentially very high-value asset. The base business's value driver is the antibiotic price spread × volume; its key risk is **structural price erosion** in commoditized cephalosporins (7-ACA ~$61/kg, range $55–65). The Exblifep value driver is successful global commercialization/licensing; its risk is **binary and early-stage** (European uptake is hampered by slow formulary inclusion; US/Japan deals "taking longer than anticipated"). Two structural features colour the whole analysis: (1) **earnings are heavily flattered by non-operating other income** — the core operating business is near-breakeven at EBIT; and (2) a **related-party merger with promoter Dhanuka Laboratories** just became effective (10-Jul-2026), which will restate share count and consolidated financials going forward.

### A3. Dhanuka Merger (161:5) — Fairness to Minorities
Promoter on both sides → conflict. Break-even test: minorities go from 30.16% of Orchid to 25.55% of (Orchid + Dhanuka standalone).
```
25.55% × (V_Orchid + V_Dhanuka) ≥ 30.16% × V_Orchid
⇒ V_Dhanuka ≥ 0.18 × V_Orchid  ⇒ ≥ ~₹966cr
```
Dhanuka standalone API business (~₹500–600cr sales, ~₹100–125cr EBITDA) ≈ ₹900–1,100cr at 8–10× — right at the break-even. Headline ₹3,540cr deal reconciles: Dhanuka's Orchid stake (~₹2,500cr, Apr-2025) + ~₹1,000cr standalone ≈ ₹3,540cr. ✓
**Verdict: broadly FAIR — mildly promoter-favorable at worst, arguably favorable (promoter took richly-priced Orchid paper).** Positives: collapses holdco layer, brings captive API/7-ACA in-house (1–2% EBITDA synergy), removes RPT-purchase leakage; protected by independent valuer (SSPA & Co.), fairness opinion, minority vote, NCLT. Caveats: backdated appointed date (1-Apr-2024); "fair" only relative to Orchid's own rich price; confirm exact issued-share count + consolidated EPS from filing. **The merger is not the problem — the price of the combined stock is.**
