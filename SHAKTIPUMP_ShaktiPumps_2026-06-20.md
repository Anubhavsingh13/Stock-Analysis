## Business Primer

Shakti Pumps (India) Ltd (Pithampur, MP) manufactures submersible pumps, motors, and solar pump systems. Revenue comes from three streams: (1) Solar pump systems (₹2–3.5 lakh/unit) supplied to state agencies under PM-KUSUM Component B — ~55% of FY26 revenue; (2) Conventional electric submersible pumps (~15%); and (3) Export sales to 100+ countries (Middle East, Africa, Southeast Asia) — ~33%. Revenue scales as volume × realization-per-pump (HP-weighted), and solar pumps carry 5–10× the realization of conventional pumps, making even a modest solar mix shift dramatically accretive. The business absolutely depends on: continued KUSUM scheme funding for domestic solar revenues, and cost-competitive manufacturing for exports.

**Business classification:** Capital-moderate manufacturer. The key value driver is product-mix (solar vs. conventional), not pure volume or price. The critical structural risk is the government receivable trap — pumps are installed under KUSUM, but SNAs (State Nodal Agencies) pay in 90–180 days, inflating working capital, suppressing FCF, and loading up net debt.
