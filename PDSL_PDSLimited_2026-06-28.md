## Business Primer

**Paragraph 1 — What the business does:**
PDS Limited is a global fashion infrastructure platform connecting fragmented manufacturing supply (600+ factories across 22 countries) with concentrated global retail demand (Ralph Lauren, Walmart, Tesco, Hanes, Kohl's, Mango, Calvin Klein among 100+ clients). Revenue flows through four streams: Design-Led Sourcing (DLS, ~88% of revenue, 3% EBIT margin) where PDS earns a commission on garment sourcing across the full product development and quality-control cycle; Sourcing-as-a-Service (~1% of revenue, 38% EBIT margin) where PDS embeds itself as the retailer's outsourced procurement department with integrated systems; Brand Management (~7%, 20% EBIT margin, operating licensed brands like Ted Baker and Forever21); and Manufacturing (~5%, 4% EBIT margin, three owned factories in Bangladesh and Sri Lanka). The company reports GMV ($2.2B in FY25) — the full transaction value — alongside reported revenue ($1.49B/₹12,578 Cr in FY25). The single most important operating driver is GMV volume growth, since PDS earns a thin 3–4% operating spread on each dollar sourced.

**Paragraph 2 — What kind of business this is:**
PDS is a capital-light orchestration platform with transaction-fee economics — structurally similar to a marketplace or staffing agency, not a manufacturer. Key value drivers are GMV volume, customer stickiness (embedded SaaS contracts), and geographic sourcing diversification. The critical risk triad: (a) customer concentration — top 20 clients ~75% of revenue; (b) manufacturing geography concentration — ~60% of sourcing flows through Bangladesh; and (c) minority interest structural drain — ~31% of consolidated PAT flows to subsidiary-level minority shareholders (the entrepreneurial CEOs who hold 15–35% equity in each subsidiary) rather than to PDSL shareholders. Two structural features colour the entire analysis: first, the 555 Vision (targeting $5B GMV, 5% PAT margins by FY29) requires both 2× revenue growth AND 4× PAT margin expansion simultaneously — ambitious requiring rigorous scrutiny; second, PDS is the only listed global apparel sourcing platform, which creates a scarcity premium that may not be fully justified at current margins.
