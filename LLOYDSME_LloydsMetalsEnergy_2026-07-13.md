## Business Primer

Lloyds Metals & Energy (LMEL) makes its money by digging iron ore out of one exceptional asset — the **Surjagarh mine in Gadchiroli, Maharashtra**, the largest single iron-ore mining operation in India. The mine was awarded by the **allocation route (not auction)**, so LMEL pays **no auction premium/revenue-share**, giving it a structural cost advantage over virtually every other Indian miner. Revenue is fundamentally **volume × realization**: the company sells high-grade (~63% Fe) direct-shipping ore and is rapidly integrating downstream into **pellets, sponge iron (DRI), power and, from FY27, finished steel** at its Konsari/Ghugus complexes. Customers are B2B — steelmakers buy the ore; internal plants consume it as LMEL builds an integrated steel value chain. The two things the business absolutely depends on: (1) continued low-cost, high-volume mining rights at Surjagarh (lease valid to **2057**, ~857 MT reserves), and (2) the global/Indian iron-ore and steel price cycle, which sets realization.

This is a **commodity, asset-heavy resource company in a hyper-growth capex phase** — not a stable compounder. The value driver is a rare combination of **all three**: volume (mining ramp from ~12 MT in FY26 toward a 26 MT FY27 target and 55 MTPA sanctioned capacity), price (iron-ore cycle), and mix (moving up the chain into pellet/DRI/steel to lift EBITDA/tonne). Three structural features colour the entire analysis: (a) **promoter-group complexity** — Thriveni Earthmovers (the mine operator, and a promoter) was brought in-house in FY26 via a large related-party transaction, so the historical mining margin now consolidates but RPT governance is central; (b) a **massive re-rating** — the stock is a ~40-bagger over four years and now sits near an all-time high on a fully-valued multiple; and (c) **operating geography risk** — Surjagarh sits in a Naxal-affected district, and the company is simultaneously venturing into overseas mining (DRC copper-cobalt, PNG copper), raising capital-allocation questions.

## Module 1 — Market, Sector & Stock Cycle

**Module 1 — Broad Market:** Nifty near record levels in mid-2026; India VIX moderate; sentiment **Optimistic-to-Neutral**, not euphoric. Metals have led a strong 6-month tape. Sizing implication: normal, with discipline given a fully-valued individual name.

**Module 1b — Sector (Metals & Mining): Tailwind, largely cyclical.**
- **Secular vs cyclical decomposition:** LMEL's reported ~155% FY26 revenue growth is overwhelmingly **company-specific volume ramp** (allocation-mine bottleneck removal + downstream commissioning), *not* sector demand. The durable structural baseline is (i) India steel-demand growth ~7–9%/yr and (ii) LMEL's own multi-year volume ramp toward 26→55 MTPA. The **realization/price component is cyclical** and mean-reverts — do NOT embed FY26 iron-ore price strength as permanent.
- **Input/competitive cycle:** LMEL is itself the low-cost supplier; its input (ore) is captive. Competitive capacity: multiple Indian miners (NMDC, integrated players) are adding, but Surjagarh's allocation-route cost position insulates it.
- **Sector verdict: Tailwind (volume-led, structural for LMEL) overlaid on a cyclical commodity price.** Treat price as cyclical in the DCF.

**Module 1c — Stock Cycle: Early/Mid-cycle, capex-heavy ramp.**
- Revenue-growth trajectory: **accelerating** (Q1FY26 ₹6,020 cr → Q4FY26 ~₹5,000 cr consol.; iron-ore output +53% YoY in Q1FY27 to 6.05 MT — annualising ~24 MT vs 26 MT target). Buy-signal on the operational metric.
- Revenue decomposition: **volume-dominant** (most durable while ramping to a capacity ceiling of 55 MTPA), aided by rising value-add mix (DRI +131% YoY).
- Physical capacity model: Surjagarh EC granted for **55 MTPA** (June 2025 / expanded clearance May 2026); reserves 857 MT ⇒ >20yr life ⇒ the volume runway is real and permitted.
- Classification: **Early-cycle / Ramp-up** — margins strong but capex heavy, FCF negative during build-out. Standard MoS; FCF inflection likely FY28–29 once the ₹13,500 cr capex tapers.

## Module 4 — Management & Capital Allocation

**Promoter holding:** 61.65% (Mar-2026), down from 63.44% (Mar-2025) — a ~1.8pp decline, partly QIP dilution/reclassification. **Pledge trend: improving** — Thriveni Earthmovers has been *releasing* pledges on LMEL shares (multiple 2025–26 pledge-release filings) — a positive.

**Capital allocation — MIXED / a genuine concern:**
- ✅ **Thriveni consolidation (Jul-2025):** LMEL acquired 79.82% of Thriveni Earthmovers & Infra (the MDO/mine operator). Strategically sound — it **internalises the mining margin** that previously leaked to a promoter-related contractor, and it consolidates that EBITDA. But it is a **massive RPT** (a ₹15,820 cr material-RPT postal ballot passed Apr-2026 with 98.19% votes), so minority-fairness scrutiny is paramount.
- 🔴 **QIP-proceeds leakage flag:** A monitoring report flagged **₹432.61 cr paid to related parties** for the pellet-plant project from QIP/preferential proceeds, including **₹196.61 cr to a promoter entity (TEMPL)** — which *appears to contradict the placement document's assurance that promoters would not indirectly receive proceeds.* Documentation reportedly lacked formal scope/timeline agreements. **This is the most serious governance flag.**
- 🔴 **ICD of ₹841 cr to Pune IT Space Solutions Pvt Ltd** — capital parked in a non-core, unrelated entity; capital-efficiency/liquidity question.
- 🟠 **Cost overrun:** 4 MTPA pellet plant ₹1,625 cr → ₹2,236 cr (+₹611 cr, ~38%); DRI/power expansion delayed 9+ months.
- 🟠 **Overseas empire-building:** 49% stake in **Chemaf (DRC copper-cobalt)** and a **Panguna (PNG) copper** push. Far outside core competence, high-risk jurisdictions — classic late-stage diversification risk.
- ✅ Dividend initiated (Re 1/share FY26 — token; company is rightly reinvesting).

**Buffett Three C's:** Competence — high (operational execution excellent). Candor — moderate (disclosures exist but RPT documentation gaps). **Caring — the weak link** (promoter-entity payments against placement assurances; ICD to unrelated entity).
