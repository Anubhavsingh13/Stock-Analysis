## Business Primer
Laxmipati Engineering Works (formerly L.P. Naval and Engineering Ltd) is a Gujarat-based small engineering company operating in two segments: (1) Fabrication — heavy fabrication, engineering infrastructure, and shutdown maintenance projects for industrial clients; (2) Shipyard — ship repair, ship building support, and precision machining. Revenue for FY26 was ₹71.99 Cr, up 44% from ₹50.09 Cr in FY25 — reasonable organic growth. However, net worth is only ₹35.61 Cr, and the company's PAT of ₹27 Cr in FY26 was inflated by a ₹23.68 Cr one-time gain from an asset sale — the company effectively sold assets worth more than 6 months of revenue and booked the gain as profit. Recurring PAT was approximately ₹3.4 Cr. The company is listed on BSE and appears to benefit from spillover retail interest in the defence/shipbuilding theme. 52-week high ₹452.3 vs. current ₹304 = already corrected 33% from peak.

This is a commodity fabrication business — no pricing power, no long-term contracts disclosed, no differentiated technology. Key dependencies are: (a) proximity to Gujarat's industrial/shipyard ecosystem for fabrication work and (b) winning maintenance/repair contracts from larger yards and port operators. At current scale, the company competes on price with dozens of similar small fabrication shops across Gujarat.
