## Executive Summary

Power Mech Projects is India's largest Operations & Maintenance (O&M) services provider for power plants, with an integrated presence across erection/commissioning, civil works, and the emerging Mine Development & Operations (MDO) segment. The business has compounded revenue at ~16% over 10 years and is in the early stages of a high-margin MDO ramp-up that could structurally re-rate earnings from FY28 onward. The stock trades at ~21x TTM PE — fairly valued relative to peers — with a base-case intrinsic value of ~₹3,600–3,800, offering moderate upside; however, management has consistently missed order inflow and revenue guidance by 15–28%, the promoter stake has declined ~6% over three years, and the March 2025 operating cash flow near-zero despite ₹348 Cr PAT was a forensic yellow flag. Enter on technical confirmation above the Stage 2 breakout level with a defined stop.

**Verdict:** ~70% of revenue growth is secular (structural aging O&M + coal MDO mandate). ~30% is cyclical (government capex timing). The MDO component is entirely secular with 25-28 year contract horizons.

### Key Sector Dynamics

**Tailwinds:**
- India's coal-based generation runs at ~70% of capacity and requires continuous O&M — Power Mech is embedded in this ecosystem
- 25+ GW of new thermal capacity planned over FY26–30 (800 MW supercritical plants); Balance of Plant EPC is a target segment
- Coal India's mine expansion mandate (domestic coal > imports) → MDO demand structural
- India's power demand growing 6–8%/yr; O&M is the last-mile essential service

**Headwinds:**
- Long-term transition risk: renewable energy growth could slow new thermal orders post FY30
- Government client (NTPC, SAIL, Coal India) → payment terms are slow (debtor days 90-102)
- Competitive bidding in EPC erection contracts → limited pricing power
- Simultaneous capacity build among peers (Thermax, L&T PowerTech, Triveni Engineering)

**Sector Cycle Verdict: Strong Tailwind** — Power sector investment cycle is firmly in upswing; MDO is a structural tailwind

## Module 3 — Business Quality & Moat

### Greenwald's Three Barriers Test

**1. Supply/Cost Advantage:**
- Scale in O&M (claimed to be largest O&M service provider in India) provides some cost advantage through trained manpower pools and equipment utilization
- NOT a decisive cost advantage — competitors can build manpower over time
- **Result: Partial/Weak**

**2. Customer Captivity (Switching Costs):**
- O&M: Once a company is managing a plant's operations (turbine maintenance, safety protocols, trained teams), switching is costly and risky for the plant operator. Safety and continuity risks keep customers sticky.
- MDO: 25–28 year contracts with Coal India and SAIL — essentially permanent customer captivity for contract duration
- Long-term O&M contracts with SAIL (₹30,383 Cr/28 yr) and Coal India (₹9,294 Cr/25 yr)
- **Result: REAL MOAT in O&M and MDO segments** (switching cost + long-duration contracts)

**3. Local Scale Economies:**
- Dominant within the power plant O&M niche — claimed market leader
- Geographic coverage across India (not just a regional player)
- **Result: Partial** — scale matters but not a hard local monopoly

**Moat Verdict: NARROW MOAT** — Primarily from switching costs in long-duration O&M/MDO contracts; absent in competitive EPC erection segment.

**Buffett Moat Test:** Can raise prices 10% without losing significant volume? In PSU O&M contracts, pricing is negotiated on renewal, with some pricing power on the incumbent's side (switching cost). In open EPC tenders, NO pricing power. **PARTIAL PASS.**
