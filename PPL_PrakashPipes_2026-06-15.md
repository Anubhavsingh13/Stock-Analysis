## Executive Summary

Prakash Pipes Limited (NSE: PPL, BSE: 542684) is a small-cap manufacturer of PVC pipes & fittings (~55% revenue) and flexible packaging (~45% revenue), headquartered in Kashipur, Uttarakhand. The stock has collapsed ~47% from its Dec-2024 peak of ~₹490 to ₹260, driven entirely by a sector-wide margin compression in FY26 — EBITDA margin fell from 15.5% (FY25) to 9.6% (FY26) due to PVC resin price volatility (global supply disruptions), unseasonal rainfall, and destocking. This was not company-specific: Apollo Pipes EBITDA fell 30%, Prince Pipes also weak. The business is nearly debt-free (₹19 Cr), promoter pledge has gone from 37% (Jun-2023) to 0% (a major governance milestone), and the promoter bought shares in the open market in March 2026. Valuation is highly compelling — 1.3x book, 6x EV/trough EBITDA, and the reverse DCF implies only ~3% perpetual growth. The case hinges on margin recovery in FY27-28 driven by PVC resin normalization, anti-dumping duty tailwind, and the packaging expansion generating higher-value revenue. The technical stage is not yet confirmed (stock in Stage 3/early Stage 4 downtrend), which is why the verdict is WAIT FOR CONFIRMATION rather than BUY — wait for a weekly close above the 30-week MA before initiating.

**Key Risk:** Flexible packaging may structurally have lower margins than PVC pipes — as the mix shifts, blended margins may not recover to historical levels of 15%+.

## MODULE 1b — Sector Cycle: PVC Pipes & Flexible Packaging

### Secular vs. Cyclical Demand Decomposition

**PVC Pipes — Demand Drivers:**
- **Secular (~60% of demand):** Urbanization, housing growth, Jal Jeevan Mission (rural water supply), PMKSY irrigation program, infrastructure capex (roads, smart cities). India's piping penetration still well below developed markets.
- **Cyclical (~40% of FY25 growth):** Post-COVID construction activity, channel restocking after the FY24 destocking, government project acceleration before elections.

**FY26 Reality:** PVC resin prices surged ~64–78% YoY in early 2026 due to Middle East geopolitical disruption → freight costs +15–25%, naphtha spike → raw material cost inflation squeezed everyone.

**Inventory Cycle:** Dealers destocked aggressively in H1 FY26 (high inventory risk from volatile resin prices) → demand suppressed even as end-market consumption held up. Restocking visible in Q4 FY26 (revenue ₹223 Cr, highest quarterly revenue in FY26).

**Competitive Capacity Pipeline:** Fragmented expansion across organized players (Apollo Pipes ₹1,000 Cr new South India plant by FY28, others also expanding). Risk of overcapacity in FY27-28 if demand doesn't keep pace.

**Anti-Dumping Duty (ADC) on Chinese PVC Imports (Aug 2025):** Significant positive for domestic pipe manufacturers. Reduces unfair competition from subsidized Chinese imports. Supports margin recovery as domestic resin pricing power improves for manufacturers.

**Net FX Exposure:** PVC resin is globally priced (USD). INR depreciation = input cost pressure. INR appreciation = tailwind. Prakash Pipes has limited export revenue, so net FX exposure is primarily on the input side.

**Sector Cycle Verdict:** Transitioning from Headwind (FY26) to Neutral-to-Tailwind (FY27)  
**Primary Driver:** PVC resin price normalization (transient — 1–2 quarters to stabilize), ADC protection (structural — 5-year duty period typically)

### Flexible Packaging Sector
**Secular demand:** Strong — organized food & FMCG packaging is growing at 12–15% in India driven by food processing formalization, QSR expansion, e-commerce food delivery  
**Cyclical component:** Modest; primarily secular growth  
**Sector verdict:** Tailwind — premium to PVC pipes on growth visibility
