## Executive Summary

Hester Biosciences is India's second-largest poultry vaccine manufacturer (30% market share) and the world's largest PPR vaccine producer (~75% global share) — a genuine niche compounder with a narrow but defensible moat. The business has exited a heavy 4-year capex cycle (fill-finish facility now capitalized, CWIP near zero), ROIC is recovering from a 5% trough to ~13%, and FCF turned solidly positive at ₹47 Cr in FY26. The 2-year thesis is ROIC recovery toward historical 15–18% as capacity utilisation ramps and debt is eliminated (net debt ₹70 Cr, falling toward zero by FY28). The stock has already discounted most of this recovery — rallying 62% from its 52-week low to the current ₹2,009 — leaving only 3% margin of safety to our base intrinsic value of ₹2,075. Combined with governance concerns (CEO remuneration controversy; Ravin+Bela Gandhi family stake seeking reclassification from Promoter to Public), the stock is **high quality but wrongly priced at current levels**. Buy below ₹1,550 (30% MoS) on any fundamental-unrelated correction.
