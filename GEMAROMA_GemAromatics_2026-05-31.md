## Executive Summary

Gem Aromatics (NSE: GEMAROMA) is India's dominant processor of Eugenol (62% domestic market share) and Eucalyptus Oil (58%), a specialty ingredients compounder serving oral care, pharma, cosmetics, and personal care. The company suffered a catastrophic FY26 (revenue −27%, PAT −97% to ₹1.43 Cr) driven by a clove oil input cost spike inverting Eugenol margins — a cyclical event, not structural deterioration. Simultaneously, a ₹270 Cr greenfield Dahej plant (India's largest cooling agents facility) was commissioned in December 2025, loading the P&L with incremental D&A (~₹25 Cr) and interest (~₹18–20 Cr) before revenues ramp. The investment case is a cyclical recovery story anchored by a genuine first-mover opportunity in cooling agents (WS-23/WS-3). However, ICR fell below 1× in FY26, the stock is in Weinstein Stage 4, SVAR exceeds 40%, and PIE analysis reveals asymmetry is unfavorable at the current price of ₹156.50. **Verdict: AVOID until Stage 1 base forms and ICR recovers above 2.5×.**

## Module 3 — Business Quality & Moat

**Moat Verdict:** NARROW
**Moat Source (Greenwald):** Local Economies of Scale (primary) + partial Supply Advantage
**Buffett Moat Type:** Low-Cost Scale (within India only)
**Pricing Power Test:** FAIL — FY26 demonstrated inability to protect margins against input cost spikes
**Market Share Stability (5yr):** GAINING (Eugenol 62%, Eucalyptus 58% — dominant and stable to growing)
**Fisher 15-Point Score:** ~31 / 45 *(Point 15 Integrity: Pass — no veto triggered)*

### Greenwald's Three Barriers

1. **Supply/Cost Advantage — Partial:** India's largest scale in Eugenol/Eucalyptus processing confers modest procurement advantage and operating leverage. However, the FY26 collapse proved this does NOT translate to margin protection when Indonesia sets clove oil prices at will. The cost advantage exists within India vs. smaller Indian competitors — it does NOT provide protection from the global raw material cost structure.

2. **Customer Captivity — Weak:** Pharma-grade specifications (FSSAI, CDSCO approvals for food/pharma use) create moderate stickiness — qualifying a new supplier takes 12–18 months. But buyers have global alternatives (Chinese, European Eugenol producers) and demonstrated willingness to source elsewhere during FY26 price disruption.

3. **Local Economies of Scale — REAL, NARROW:** 62% domestic Eugenol and 58% Eucalyptus market share within India represents genuine local scale dominance. Replicating this supply chain, manufacturing knowledge, and customer relationships would take a new entrant 3–5 years and ₹200+ Cr capex. This is the strongest moat element — but bounded to the Indian domestic market.

**Buffett Pricing Power Test:** FAIL. The 27% FY26 revenue decline combined with near-zero PAT demonstrates clearly that GEMAROMA cannot raise prices when input costs rise. It is a commodity-adjacent processor: it earns spread income, not brand-supported margins.

**Moat Durability — 5 Tests:**
1. ROIC vs. WACC (10yr): Insufficient public history (IPO Aug 2025). Pre-IPO ROCE ~20% reported; post-tax ROIC ~10.7% in FY25 ≈ WACC (~12%). Marginal moat at best.
2. Market share stability (5yr): Eugenol 62% — STABLE. Real barrier within India.
3. Pricing power during inflation: FAIL — FY26 is a clean natural experiment. Input inflation + no pricing power = profitability collapse.
4. Activity system: Processing → customer specification management → regulatory approvals → distribution. Somewhat reinforcing but replicable with time and capital.
5. Competitor fear: Not established from public sources (no scuttlebutt available at this stage).

**Fisher 15-Point Highlights:**
- Strong (3/3): Market potential (India oral care/FMCG growing), new product pipeline (cooling agents = genuine Point 2), scale/distribution in India
- Moderate (2/3): Management competence (Dahej bet is bold; timing was poor)
- Weak (1/3): R&D effectiveness (no proprietary IP visible), pricing power (zero)
- **Point 15 — Integrity: PASS.** Zero promoter pledge, no SEBI/MCA enforcement actions, no auditor resignation, transparent disclosures on Dahej challenges.
