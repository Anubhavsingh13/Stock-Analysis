## Business Primer
Bajaj Steel Industries Ltd, incorporated in 1961 and headquartered in Nagpur, is India's dominant manufacturer of cotton ginning and pressing machinery, commanding over 70% domestic market share and holding the distinction of being the world's leading manufacturer of Saw Ginning machinery. The company earns revenue through sale of complete ginning plant systems, individual machines, and spares to cotton gin owners — primarily B2B, project-based with a lumpy order-realisation cycle tied to India's cotton cultivation calendar. In addition to its core business, the company has two meaningful diversification legs: Pre-Engineered Buildings (PEB), where it recently expanded capacity to 35,000 MTPA and targets tripling revenues by FY28; and engineered products encompassing electrical control panels, fire-fighting systems, and hydraulic cylinders. Revenue flows as volume × realisation per system, with exports to Africa and Central Asia representing an estimated 20–30% of the machine business. The entire business chain depends on two things: (a) cotton production volumes and gin replacement cycles in India and export markets, and (b) the company's ability to execute on the PEB and engineering segments as a second growth engine.

The business is best classified as a **capital-light, niche industrial OEM** with monopoly-like domestic positioning — a structural advantage that should generate durable excess returns. The core cotton ginning segment is inherently lumpy and export-dependent, making revenues volatile; commercial clearance delays, geopolitical disruptions, and site-readiness issues at customer locations can shift significant revenue across periods. The key value driver is volume throughput on installed capacity, with pricing power constrained by the agricultural supply chain's cyclicality. The principal risks are: demand cyclicality, export disruption from tariff barriers or geopolitical friction, and the critical question of whether PEB diversification can reduce revenue volatility meaningfully. The structural feature colouring the entire analysis is the sharp FY26 deterioration — PAT dropped 56% YoY (₹84cr → ₹37cr consolidated) — alongside inventory ballooning to ₹177 crore (251 days) that demands forensic scrutiny.
