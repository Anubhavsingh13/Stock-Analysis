## Business Primer

**What the business does:**
Bajel Projects Limited (NSE: BAJEL) is a Power Transmission & Distribution (T&D) EPC contractor, demerged from Bajaj Electricals Limited in FY24 when Bajaj Electricals carved out its EPC division into a separate listed entity. Bajel operates across four verticals: Power Transmission (EHV transmission lines, substations, underground cables), Power Distribution (DISCOM-level networks), Monopoles (tubular monopoles for transmission and telecom), and International EPC (export of T&D EPC services). Revenue is project-based — the company bids for tenders from PGCIL, state DISCOMs, and private utilities, wins orders, executes over 12–36 months, and collects in tranches. The company also owns a captive manufacturing facility at Ranjangaon MIDC, Pune, producing galvanized steel structures for captive use in EPC projects; a ₹170 Cr capex is underway to expand this from ~60,000 MT to 1.1 lakh MT/year. FY26 revenue was ₹2,792 Cr, with the EPC segment driving ~90% of revenues. The business depends absolutely on: (1) a healthy order book pipeline to sustain revenue continuity, and (2) margins wide enough to cover working capital costs and service growing debt.

**What kind of business this is and what to watch:**
Bajel is a capital-light (on fixed assets), working-capital-intensive, project-based EPC business — a government-dependent infrastructure contractor in a structurally booming sector. The key value drivers are order inflow velocity, execution margin, and working capital management; all three are currently under stress. Bajel's margins (3.5% EBITDA) are 350–500 bps below KEC International (7.1%) and Kalpataru (8.3%), which execute the same government T&D tenders with the same customers. The margin gap has persisted since demerger with no specific management guidance on closure timeline. Working capital has deteriorated sharply: debtor days worsened from 136 to 214, CCC expanded from 15 to 74 days, and CFO collapsed from ₹195 Cr to ₹21 Cr even as revenue grew. The company seeks to raise its borrowing limit to ₹5,000 Cr (for BG/LC facilities as order book scales) vs current borrowings of ₹367 Cr. At P/E 114×, the market prices in full-peer-level margin delivery before the company has demonstrated ROIC above 5% in a single year.
