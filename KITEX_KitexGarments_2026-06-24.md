## Executive Summary

Kitex Garments is a declining commodity infant garment exporter masquerading as a growth story: its operating manufacturing business generated essentially zero profit in FY26 (all ₹57 Cr standalone PAT came from investment income, not garments), ROIC has collapsed from 42% to near-zero over 11 years, and management has converted the company into an investment holding company by running down the factory while growing a ₹574 Cr investment portfolio. A pending demerger will add 46% more shares, import a failed US associate (Kitex USA LLC, net worth fully eroded), and likely transfer significant subsidiary debt. At ₹168 (75.5× P/E on already-inflated PAT), the stock prices 12% annual NOPAT growth from a near-zero base — an expectation requiring 5+ years of exceptional recovery in a no-moat commodity business with a 10-year revenue CAGR of 1%.

**Three hard stops triggered: (1) ROIC persistently < WACC, (2) Upside/Downside ratio 0.2:1, (3) Earnings quality: Concerning (8/15 O'Glove flags, Sloan ratio 16.8%, auditor qualification).**

## Business Primer

Kitex Garments Ltd (KGL), incorporated in 1992 and promoted by Sabu M. Jacob from Kerala, is the world's second-largest manufacturer of cotton and organic-cotton ready-to-wear garments for infants and children aged 0–24 months. The company manufactures entirely in India (Kizhakkambalam, Kerala) and sells approximately 100% of its output as exports, overwhelmingly to the United States, supplying large US retailers and brand owners (Carter's, Gerber, Target house brands, and similar). Revenue is a function of pieces shipped × USD realisation per piece, converted at INR/USD. Kitex holds organic cotton (GOTS) certification and US CPSC compliance — real but replicable barriers. The business absolutely depends on retaining its 3–5 key US buyer relationships and maintaining competitive manufacturing costs relative to Bangladesh and Vietnam.

What the company *is* today, however, is not simply a garment manufacturer. Over the past decade, as the core business's profitability structurally deteriorated, management has been converting manufacturing assets (gross block down from ₹188 Cr net in FY15 to ₹92 Cr in FY26) into a growing investment portfolio (₹574 Cr in FY26 vs ₹35 Cr in FY20). The company is simultaneously executing a complex inbound demerger of its sister concern Kitex Childrenswear's textile division — a transaction that will add ~₹350 Cr of revenue but also import a failed US associate (Kitex USA LLC, net worth fully eroded, persistent auditor qualification) and 9.24 crore new shares (46% dilution for existing holders). The key structural risk is that Kitex has become partly an investment holding company wearing a garment manufacturer's clothes — a form of capital misallocation where an operating business slowly hollows out while treasury income masks the erosion.
