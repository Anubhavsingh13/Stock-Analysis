## Executive Summary

BigBloc Construction is India's only listed pure-play AAC (Autoclaved Aerated Concrete) block manufacturer — a structurally growing segment that benefits from India's urbanisation and green-building shift. However, aggressive capacity expansion into an increasingly crowded market has triggered a catastrophic margin collapse: EBITDA margins fell from ~25% in FY23/FY24 to ~8% in FY26, and the company recorded a net loss of ₹9 crore in FY26 despite 26% revenue growth. With Debt/EBITDA at ~11.5× and Interest Coverage at ~1.4× — both triggering hard-stop thresholds — the stock fails two mandatory gates. Management's FY27–28 targets of ₹428 cr revenue and ₹125 cr EBITDA require a 5× EBITDA jump in two years from a loss-making base, in an industry with structural overcapacity. The stock has fallen ~40% YoY and is trading near its 52-week low. The only genuine positives are zero promoter pledge with a consistent 5-year stake increase, and an accelerating volume trajectory in Q4 FY26 (+40% YoY). Not enough to overcome the financial distress signals.

## Module 9 — Valuation: PIE First, Then DCF

### 9A. Price-Implied Expectations (Mauboussin PIE)

**Inputs:**
- CMP: ~₹49 | Shares: ~141 mn | Market Cap: ~₹690 cr
- Net Debt (est.): Debt/EBITDA 11.5× × ₹23 cr = ~₹264 cr; less minimal cash
- **EV: ~₹954 cr**
- NOPAT (FY26): Negative; EBIT ≈ ₹23 cr − ₹20 cr depreciation = ₹3 cr → NOPAT ≈ ₹2 cr (after ~30% tax)
- WACC: ~12%

**Reverse DCF — What the market is pricing in:**

Using simplified PIE: `g = (EV × WACC − NOPAT₀) / (EV + NOPAT₀)`
```
g = (954 × 12% − 2) / (954 + 2)
g = (114.5 − 2) / 956
g ≈ 11.8% perpetual NOPAT growth
```

But this is on a near-zero NOPAT base — the market is NOT pricing perpetual 11.8% growth. The market is pricing in a **complete recovery AND substantial margin expansion**. Back-solving two-stage:

| PIE Driver | Market-Implied | My Base Case | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue CAGR (5-yr) | ~20–25% | ~18–20% | Small — volume story accepted |
| NOPAT Margin (steady-state) | ~12–14% | ~8–10% | **Market too optimistic on margins** |
| Investment Rate (Indore + chemicals) | ~50–60% | ~60–70% (heavy capex) | Aligned |

**Expectations Gap:** Market is pricing in margin recovery to 12–14% NOPAT. My assessment is that 8–10% NOPAT steady-state (implying EBITDA ~15–18%) is more realistic given industry structure with NO moat and competitive overcapacity. At 8–10% NOPAT margins on ₹350 cr revenue (FY28 est.), NOPAT ≈ ₹28–35 cr → at 15× EV/NOPAT → EV ≈ ₹420–525 cr → Equity value ≈ ₹160–265 cr → per share ₹11–19. **The market at ₹49 is pricing in a far more optimistic recovery.**

**Expectations Treadmill:** For this stock to outperform its own PIE, NOPAT margin must reach and sustain >12% — requiring realization per CBM to recover to ₹3,500–3,800 AND capacity utilization at 85%+. This requires industry pricing discipline from all major players (unlikely given overcapacity) or a material demand surge.

