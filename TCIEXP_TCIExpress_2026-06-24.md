## Business Primer

TCI Express Limited provides express delivery solutions across India operating primarily as a B2B surface express carrier. The company earns revenue by moving time-definite, part-load cargo between businesses — picking up consignments from sender premises, routing them through 28+ sorting centres, and delivering to consignees across 60,000+ locations through 970+ company-owned branches. Revenue flows as realization per kilogram × total kilograms handled; the business is therefore a function of two levers — volume (how many tonnes move through the network) and yield (how much the company earns per kg). Surface Express accounts for ~81% of revenue; the remaining ~19% comes from newer multimodal verticals: Rail Express (~8%), Domestic/International Air Express (~7%), C2C Express (~3%), and Cold Chain/E-commerce Express (nascent). The single most important dependency is the productivity of its fixed-cost sort-center and branch network — once that infrastructure is built, every additional kilogram contributes almost entirely to EBITDA, giving the model powerful operating leverage.

The business is best classified as a capital-light compounder with a network moat — once a nationwide branch-and-sort-center network exists at the required density, replicating it takes 8–10 years and several hundred crores of capital, creating a genuine barrier. The key value driver over the next three years is the intersection of volume recovery (the company handled flat revenue for FY23–26 as post-COVID demand normalised) and realization improvement (yield management returning pricing power after a competitive trough). The primary structural risk is margin compression: EBITDA margins have nearly halved from ~16% (FY23) to ~11.7% (FY26), driven by fuel cost inflation, competitive pricing pressure, and underutilized new sort-center capacity. The critical question this analysis must answer is whether this margin compression is cyclical and reversible — or structural and permanent. A second feature colouring the entire analysis is the ₹400 Cr capex cycle (FY23–FY27) that is suppressing near-term FCF but will determine the company's network capacity for the next decade.
