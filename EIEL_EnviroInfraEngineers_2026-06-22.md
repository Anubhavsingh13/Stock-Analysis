## BUSINESS PRIMER

Enviro Infra Engineers Ltd is a water and wastewater treatment EPC (Engineering, Procurement & Construction) company that also operates under long-term O&M (Operations & Maintenance) contracts. The company designs, builds, and operates sewage treatment plants (STPs), water treatment plants (WTPs), effluent treatment plants (ETPs), and water supply/distribution infrastructure primarily for government clients — municipal bodies, state governments, and central government agencies like NMCG and AMRUT. Revenue flows through two streams: lump-sum EPC project execution (one-time, high-margin recognition over project life) and recurring O&M annuities (stable, lower-capital, high-cash). Founded in 1996 by Sanjay Jain (CMD) and Manish Jain (MD), the company IPO'd in November 2024 at ₹148. The business absolutely depends on two things: government budgetary allocation to water infrastructure (AMRUT 2.0, Jal Jeevan Mission, NMCG), and the company's ability to execute projects without cost overruns in a government-customer environment notorious for payment delays and retention money holds.

The company is best classified as an asset-light government EPC contractor with a growing annuity tail. The key value driver is order book size × execution velocity — the faster EIEL converts its ₹6,814 Cr order book into revenues and then into cash, the more intrinsic value it creates. The key risk is working capital: government clients typically withhold 10–15% of contract value as retention money until project completion, creating a structural gap between reported profits and operating cash flow. A secondary risk is the company's strategic pivot into renewable energy EPC (Suyog Urja acquisition for ₹311 Cr wind EPC + 930 MWH BESS from NTPC) — execution discipline in an unfamiliar segment is untested. One structural feature colours the entire analysis: EIEL has persistently negative operating cash flow despite reporting healthy profits, explained almost entirely by retention money build and unbilled revenue accumulation on long-cycle government projects. This is not manipulation — but it is a material constraint on the business's ability to self-fund and pay dividends until projects reach completion milestones.

### Secular vs. Cyclical Decomposition

**Secular (structural, durable — embed in DCF):**
- AMRUT 2.0: ₹2.87 lakh crore outlay for urban water/sewage infrastructure over 2021–26 — largest ever Indian urban water scheme
- Jal Jeevan Mission Phase 2: tap water to every rural household; ₹3.60 lakh crore pipeline

- India Water TAM: USD 9.64B (2024) → USD 18.6B (2033) at 7.6% CAGR — structural, not cyclical
- Urbanisation rate: 36% → 50% expected by 2040; each 1% urban population gain = 10–15 Mn new municipal water connections
- Regulatory mandates: Supreme Court orders on sewage treatment; NGT fines on untreated discharge — non-negotiable compliance driver

**Cyclical (temporary, mean-reverts — do NOT embed as permanent):**
- Government election-year ordering burst (FY24/FY25 saw elevated ordering pre-election); may moderate post-election
- Partial post-COVID pent-up ordering catch-up in municipal capex — largely played out

**Verdict:** EIEL's 53% revenue CAGR (FY20–FY26) contains both secular demand pull AND early-mover capture of market share from ₹0. The structural baseline forward is 20–25% revenue growth — secular + market-share gain — NOT 53%. Embedding 53% would massively overvalue.

### Input Cost Cycle
EPC contracts are largely fixed-price; raw material (steel, cement, electrical equipment) inflation erodes margins. FY26 saw commodity softening — mild tailwind. Forward: stable.

### Competitive Capacity Pipeline
Water treatment EPC is not capacity-constrained in the same way as manufacturing. Competition is for project wins, not physical capacity. Key competitors: VA Tech Wabag, Ion Exchange, Thermax, Patel Engineering. EIEL wins on price competitiveness and government relationship depth. No structural oversupply signal.

**Sector Cycle Verdict: STRONG TAILWIND** — 10+ year secular driver, government-mandated, underserved market. Duration: structural, not cyclical.

## MODULE 8 — Valuation

### 8A. Mauboussin PIE (Price-Implied Expectations)

**Inputs:**
- EV = MCap ₹3,778 Cr + Debt ₹179 Cr − Cash ₹300 Cr = **₹3,657 Cr**
- NOPAT₀ = EBIT × (1 − tax) = ₹207 Cr × 0.75 = **₹155 Cr**
- WACC = **13%** (Rf 7% + ERP 6% × Beta 1.0 = 13%; modest beta given government contracts)
- RONIC = **16%** (current ROIC; assume market prices continuation)

**PIE formula:**
g = (EV × WACC − NOPAT₀) / (EV + NOPAT₀/RONIC)
g = (3,657 × 0.13 − 155) / (3,657 + 155/0.16)
g = (475 − 155) / (3,657 + 969)
g = 320 / 4,626
**g = 6.9% perpetual implied growth**

**Expectations Gap Analysis:**
- PIE implies market expects only 6.9% perpetual NOPAT growth
- Company's actual 3-year NOPAT CAGR: ~40%+
- Order book at 5.95× revenue signals at minimum 30–40% revenue growth for 2 years
- India water market growing 7.6% — EIEL should at least grow 3–4× market (20–25% sustainable)
- **PIE gap: MASSIVELY PESSIMISTIC** — market is pricing in almost no growth premium

**Conclusion from PIE:** The market is not pricing in the order book, the growth trajectory, or the sector tailwind. This is an **expectations gap opportunity**.
