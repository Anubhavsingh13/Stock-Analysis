## Business Primer

Tejas Networks is a Tata Sons-backed Indian telecom equipment OEM that designs, develops, and manufactures optical networking, broadband, and wireless (4G/5G RAN) products sold to telecom operators, governments, ISPs, utilities, and defence agencies across 75 countries. Revenue flows on a project/contract basis — equipment delivered against specific purchase orders — not on a recurring subscription model. The company's economics are overwhelmingly dominated by a small number of large government contracts: the BSNL 4G rollout program was essentially the entirety of FY25 revenue, making Tejas the single-vendor supplier for India's 100,000-site public 4G network, driving revenue from ₹2,471 Cr in FY24 to ₹8,923 Cr in FY25. Secondary revenue streams include IP/MPLS routers for BharatNet Phase 3 (Tejas won 7 of 12 packages), optical DWDM gear for private telecom carriers, and a nascent 5G international business through a co-development partnership with NEC Corporation. The company has filed 676+ patents and employs 65%+ of its workforce in R&D.

Tejas is best classified as a high-working-capital government-contract OEM — asset-light in fixed plant but extremely working-capital-intensive during large project execution. The key value driver is order inflow diversification and order-to-cash conversion velocity. The central risk is customer concentration: BSNL's absence in FY26 caused revenue to collapse 88% YoY to ₹1,103 Cr and produced a net loss of ₹909 Cr, with gross debt reaching ₹4,035 Cr against a cash cushion of ₹505 Cr. The structural debate is whether Tejas can evolve from a government-contract cyclical into a diversified telecom equipment platform (private carriers, BharatNet, international 5G via NEC), a transformation requiring sustained multi-year R&D investment precisely when the company is under acute financial stress.
