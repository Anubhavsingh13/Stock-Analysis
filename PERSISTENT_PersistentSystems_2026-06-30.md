## Executive Summary

Persistent Systems announced the acquisition of Germany-listed Nagarro SE via a voluntary public takeover offer at EUR 81/share (140% premium to undisturbed price), financed by EUR 1.4B debt from Barclays. The stock fell ~11% on announcement. The market is misreading the deal: the absolute acquisition multiple is ~7× EV/Adj EBITDA — roughly half the typical IT M&A multiple — because Nagarro was deeply undervalued on Frankfurt (trading at 2.25× EV/EBITDA pre-deal). Post-close leverage peaks at ~2.8× Net Debt/EBITDA, decelerating rapidly given ~$350M combined OCF. The 10% selloff is forced selling by pure-play mandate funds, not a fundamental deterioration — creating a special situation entry at 35× FY26 EPS for a combined entity that will compound EPS at 20%+ through FY28.

## Business Primer

Persistent Systems is a mid-cap Indian IT services and digital engineering company listed on NSE/BSE. It earns revenue primarily through software product engineering services, application development, and AI-led technology solutions delivered to enterprise clients across North America (~80% of revenue), Europe, and Rest of World. Revenue is project- and contract-based in USD, with clients concentrated in BFSI, healthcare, and technology verticals. The business depends on two things to earn its returns: winning and retaining large multi-year technology contracts, and maintaining the talent supply to deliver them at acceptable margins.

This is a capital-light, people-intensive compounder — a business where the primary asset walks out the door every evening. Persistent has been among the fastest-growing mid-cap IT names in India over FY23–26, compounding revenue at 27%+ annually, with EBIT margins expanding to 15.6%. The key risk going forward is whether management can absorb a transformative EUR 1.4 billion leveraged acquisition of Nagarro SE without disrupting the operating momentum of the Persistent mothership. The acquisition fundamentally changes the financial profile: from net-cash, high-ROIC compounder to a levered, cross-border integration story.

**Critical insight:** Nagarro was trading at EUR 33.75 on Frankfurt — a market cap of ~EUR 436M and EV of ~EUR 311M on a company doing €999M of revenue and €138M adj EBITDA. That is EV/Revenue of 0.31× and EV/Adj EBITDA of 2.25×. Persistent found a genuinely cheap asset.

**Acquisition Multiple (what Persistent actually pays):**

```
Equity value paid    = EUR 81 × 12.92M = EUR 1,046M
Estimated Nagarro debt = EUR ~50M
Cash acquired        = EUR 124.6M
EV paid              = EUR 1,046M + EUR 50M − EUR 124.6M = EUR ~971M

Acquisition EV/Revenue       = EUR 971M / EUR 999M = 0.97×
Acquisition EV/Adj EBITDA    = EUR 971M / EUR 138M = 7.0×
```

**Comparable IT M&A multiples:**

| Deal | EV/Revenue | EV/EBITDA |
|---|---|---|
| Wipro / Capco (2021) | ~1.4× | ~15× |
| HCL / Actian (2023) | ~1.5× | ~12× |
| Accenture typical | 1–2× | 12–18× |
| **Persistent / Nagarro (2026)** | **0.97×** | **7.0×** |

**Verdict: 7× EV/Adj EBITDA is roughly half the typical IT M&A multiple. The 140% premium is to a Frankfurt-undervalued stock, not to intrinsic value.**
