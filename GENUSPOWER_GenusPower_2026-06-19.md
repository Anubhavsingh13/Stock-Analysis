## Business Primer

Genus Power Infrastructures Ltd is a Jaipur-based company with two interlinked revenue engines. The first is smart meter manufacturing: it designs and fabricates advanced metering infrastructure (AMI) devices at an 18-million-unit annual capacity facility, selling meters to electricity DISCOMs and to AMISPs. The second — and structurally dominant — engine is its role as an AMISP (Advanced Metering Infrastructure Service Provider) under the government's Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme (RDSS). In the AMISP model (DBFOOT: Design-Build-Finance-Operate-Transfer), Genus finances, installs, and operates the complete smart meter + communication + IT infrastructure for a DISCOM over an 8–9 year concession period, earning a monthly per-meter service charge throughout. Revenue consequently flows from two streams: upfront installation revenue (meters deployed × average realization of ~₹473/meter, which is driving the current explosive topline at ₹4,737 Cr in FY26) and a slowly accumulating recurring service revenue tail on the cumulative installed base of 10+ million meters. The business depends absolutely on two things: the DISCOMs paying monthly service charges reliably over the entire concession life, and Genus maintaining execution capability to install millions of meters per year without cost overruns.

Genus Power is best classified as a hybrid infrastructure concession + capital goods manufacturing company — the financial profile resembles a road concession (high upfront investment, debt-funded, negative OCF during deployment, with long-dated cash flows) rather than a conventional manufacturer. The critical structural feature to understand before any analysis is the GIC (Singapore sovereign wealth fund) partnership: GIC holds 74% and Genus holds 26% of the AMISP concession platform, with Genus as the exclusive meter supplier. This means Genus earns a manufacturing margin on every meter sold to the platform AND 26% of the concession economics — but most AMISP capital sits in GIC-owned SPVs off Genus's main balance sheet. GIC also invested ₹519 Cr directly in Genus via warrants, taking a 15% equity stake, diluting promoters from 50% to 39%. The key risks are government counterparty risk (DISCOM payment reliability), raw material cost pressures (copper, semiconductors, FX), and the margin profile guided downward (20.5% FY26 → 18% FY27).
