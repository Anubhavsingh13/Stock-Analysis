## Executive Summary

Nureca sells home-healthcare devices (Dr Trust, Dr Physio, Trumom) that it designs but does not make — it imports, brands, and sells them on Amazon and Flipkart. It is a genuine post-COVID wreck now staging a real operating recovery: FY26 revenue ₹147 Cr (+34%), first positive EBITDA (₹8.28 Cr) in four years, and Q1 FY27 revenue +18% YoY with PAT ₹3.07 Cr. **The recovery is real. The investment case is not.** Three things break it: (1) the business has no moat — zero pricing power on a marketplace, imported product identical to competitors', and a device portfolio being actively substituted by smartwatches; (2) reported earnings are a treasury artefact — other income of ₹8 Cr exceeded operating EBIT, and Q4 FY26's ₹6.6 Cr loss was purely a mark-to-market hit on investments; and (3) management has just committed **₹128 Cr of capex — 42% of market cap and effectively the entire treasury** — into greenfield manufacturing with no disclosed customer, having six months earlier bought back stock at ₹330 (1.75× book, ~150× earnings). The key risk is that the capex simultaneously destroys the other income that currently *is* the profit and adds ~₹12 Cr of annual depreciation, requiring EBITDA to more than double just to stand still.

## BUSINESS PRIMER

Nureca is a digitally-native consumer healthcare company that designs and sells home-health devices under its own brands — **Dr Trust** (BP monitors, pulse oximeters, nebulisers, thermometers, weighing scales), **Dr Physio** (massagers, physiotherapy devices) and **Trumom** (mother-and-baby care) — across roughly 150+ SKUs. It is a brand-and-distribution business, not a manufacturer: products are designed in-house and contract-manufactured (predominantly China-sourced), then sold B2C through online marketplaces (Amazon, Flipkart), its own website, and a growing offline/modern-trade channel. Revenue is straightforward volume × realisation, with no order book, no contracts and no recurring revenue — every rupee has to be re-won each quarter through ad spend, marketplace ranking and price. The two things the business absolutely depends on are (a) **marketplace demand-generation economics** — whether customer acquisition cost stays below gross margin — and (b) **landed product cost from Chinese vendors**, which sets the entire gross margin ceiling.

This is a **capital-light branded-consumer business currently attempting to become an asset-heavy manufacturer**. Three structural features colour everything. First, it is a **post-COVID melt-down case**: revenue peaked at ₹256 Cr in FY22 with ₹45 Cr PAT, collapsed to ₹111 Cr with a ₹15 Cr operating loss in FY23, and FY26's ₹147 Cr remains 43% below peak — so all "growth" is recovery off a broken base, not compounding. Second, **a large IPO-funded treasury distorts reported earnings**: other income of ₹8–12 Cr per year has exceeded operating profit in every year since FY23, and Q4 FY26 swung to a ₹6.6 Cr net loss purely on an ₹8.34 Cr fair-value loss on investments — the P&L is currently more a fund statement than a business statement. Third, **working capital is extreme** — inventory days of 187–214 and a cash conversion cycle of 186 days, which is why free cash flow has been negative in four of the last five years despite an asset-light model.

## Module 1b — Sector Cycle: Tailwinds & Headwinds

### Secular vs. Cyclical Demand Decomposition — the single most important step here

FY26 revenue growth was **+34%** (₹109.7 Cr → ₹147.0 Cr). Decomposed:

| Component | Estimate | Durability |
|---|---|---|
| **Secular** — India home-diagnostics/wellness category growth (diabetes and hypertension prevalence, ageing, preventive-care habit formation) | **~12–14%** | Durable — embed in DCF |
| **Cyclical / base recovery** — normalisation off a collapsed FY24–FY25 base, distribution rebuild, SKU rationalisation | **~20–22%** | **Fades within 4–6 quarters — do NOT embed** |

**The fade is already visible.** Q1 FY27 revenue growth was **+18.0% YoY**, down from FY26's +34%. On the skill's own warning: a company reporting 34% growth that is 13% secular plus 21% recovery mean-reverts to ~12–14% once the base effect washes through. **Anchor the DCF to 12–14%, not 34%.**

### Other Sector Cycle Inputs

- **Input cost cycle:** Chinese electronics/component costs stable; sea freight elevated on Red Sea re-routing. Neutral-to-slightly-negative.
- **Competitive capacity pipeline:** ⚠️ **Adverse.** PLI for medical devices plus import-substitution policy is pushing multiple asset-light Indian brands into domestic manufacturing simultaneously in the FY26–FY28 window. Classic capital-cycle oversupply setup — capital flooding into a category that has not demonstrated it can earn returns.
- **Geopolitical / sourcing risk:** **Structural, not transient.** Predominantly China-sourced product across the full SKU range. Any tariff action, QCO enforcement, or customs tightening is a sourcing-redesign problem, not a quarter's re-routing. Widen MoS.
- **Net FX exposure:** Revenue in INR, COGS in USD → **large negative net FX exposure**. Estimated imported COGS ₹65–70 Cr. A 5% INR depreciation ≈ ₹3.4 Cr cost increase = **~41% of FY26 EBITDA**. Margin is partly an FX bet.

**Sector cycle verdict: TAILWIND on demand, HEADWIND on structure.** The category grows 12–15% and is real. But regulation, capital inflow, and substitution are all moving against incumbent economics. Net: **NEUTRAL, requiring a 5–10pp wider MoS** for the China-sourcing structural risk.

### Operating Leverage — this is the fragility

```
Gross margin (est. ~30%) → Contribution ≈ ₹44 Cr
EBIT (FY26)                        = ₹6.3 Cr
DOL = 44 / 6.3                     = 7.0×
DFL = 6.3 / (6.3 − 1.0)            = 1.19×
DTL = 7.0 × 1.19                   = 8.3×
```
**A 5% revenue miss produces a ~42% EPS miss.** ICR is not a concern (near-zero debt, ICR ~6×), but the operating fragility is severe and is exactly what Q4 FY26 demonstrated — a ₹4 Cr sequential revenue decline flipped ₹2.4 Cr of operating profit into a ₹6.5 Cr operating loss.

### FCF Inflection Year

**Pushed out, not approaching.** FCF was negative in FY22, FY24, FY25 and FY26. The ₹128 Cr capex programme guarantees deeply negative FCF through FY27–FY29. **FCF inflection: FY30 at the earliest, and only if the plant fills.**
