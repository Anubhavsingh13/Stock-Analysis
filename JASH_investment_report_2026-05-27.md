## 3. Business Quality & Moat

**Moat Verdict: Narrow — Switching Costs + Niche Scale**

Jash makes custom-engineered water control gates, penstocks, stop logs, screens, and aeration equipment for water/wastewater treatment plants, power stations, steel mills, and desalination facilities. Moat sources:

1. **Switching costs post-vendor approval:** Government utilities and international clients re-use approved vendors. Re-qualification is time-consuming and costly. Once in a project, subsequent phases typically return to the same supplier.
2. **Niche scale + experience:** 75+ years of operating history (Jash founded 1948); Rodney Hunt (USA) operational since 1840; Mahr Maschinenbau (Austria) since 1927. Cumulative engineering knowledge and reference installations are genuine barriers.
3. **International certification moat:** Operating in 45 countries across multiple regulatory regimes requires sustained compliance investment; a new entrant cannot replicate this in under 5–7 years.

**Buffett moat test:** Can Jash raise prices 5% in India government tenders? Not easily (L1 bidding). Can it in the US/Europe/Gulf? Yes — certified suppliers command a modest premium over unqualified alternatives. Partial pass.

**Moat durability:** Market share > 70% in India water control gates segment (confirmed by multiple sources). ROCE averaging 14–18% over the cycle — above WACC but not dramatically so. Consistent with a narrow (not wide) moat.

## 9. Valuation — PIE First, Then DCF

### 9A. Price-Implied Expectations (Updated at ₹467)

**EV Calculation:**
```
Market Cap: ₹2,948 Cr
Net Cash (net D/E = −0.02; equity ~₹519 Cr → net cash ~₹10 Cr)

g = (EV × WACC − NOPAT) / (EV + NOPAT)
= (2938 × 0.12 − 76.5) / (2938 + 76.5)
= (352.6 − 76.5) / 3014.5
= 276.1 / 3014.5
```
