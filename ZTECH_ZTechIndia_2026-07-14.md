## Executive Summary
Z-Tech is a fast-growing micro-cap (₹844cr, listed on NSE-SME only in June 2024 at ₹110, now ~₹580) that builds geotechnical/civil works, industrial wastewater plants, and — its dominant, "high-margin" line — **"sustainable theme parks" made from recycled scrap** (the ZinkPark platform, ~60–70% of revenue). On the surface it looks spectacular: FY26 revenue ₹156cr (+65%), PAT ₹36cr (+83%), 28% margins, 20% ROCE. Underneath, the accounts throw a **decisive red flag: the company reported +₹56cr of cumulative profit over FY25–FY26 while burning −₹129cr of operating cash** — the "earnings" are sitting in receivables (debtor days 220 and rising) and work-in-progress, funded by IPO cash, warrant conversions, and a jump in borrowings (₹2cr → ₹75cr). Abnormally high margins for a construction business, extreme accruals (Sloan ratio ~35–40% vs a +10% red-flag line), a novel/hard-to-verify core product, near-zero institutional ownership, and a tiny post-IPO float together trip multiple framework hard stops. Whatever the eventual reality, this is un-investable on a disciplined earnings-quality basis until cash conversion is proven. **AVOID.**

## BUSINESS PRIMER
Z-Tech (India) operates three lines: **Geo-Technical Solutions ("Terra"** — specialty civil/foundation engineering, ground improvement for infrastructure), **Industrial Waste Water Management ("ACWA"** — sewage treatment, wastewater recycling EPC), and **Sustainable Theme / "Creative" Park Development ("ZinkPark"** — building public parks and themed installations using recycled scrap/waste materials, largely for government and municipal clients). The third line is the economic engine, ~60–70% of revenue, and is described as the "high-margin" business. Revenue is essentially **project-based / EPC** — recognized on percentage-of-completion as milestones are certified — with a stated strategic shift toward a "hybrid" model that adds recurring/annuity income from operating the parks (ZinkPark). Customers are predominantly government/municipal bodies; the order book is ~₹168cr+.

This is a **tiny, recently-listed, project-based EPC/construction company with a novel and largely unproven "green parks" concept at its core** — and it must be read primarily through the lens of *earnings quality and cash conversion*, not growth. The key value driver management sells is high-margin park EPC plus a recurring-revenue ramp; the key risks are (1) **revenue/margin recognition quality** — abnormally high margins in a construction business, recognized on % -completion, while cash does not arrive; (2) **government-receivable and working-capital risk** — debtor days of 220 and deeply negative operating cash flow; and (3) **micro-cap / newly-listed governance and liquidity risk** — a ~1.45cr-share float, ~2,263 shareholders, minimal institutional ownership, promoter dilution, and a share price up ~5× from its IPO in two years. Every "growth" number in this analysis must be checked against whether the cash ever showed up. It did not.

## Module 11 — Forward View

**Why the framework says AVOID anyway:** Investing requires the reported profits to be *real cash* within a reasonable horizon. Here, two consecutive years show large, growing paper profits and large, persistent cash burn, with margins that are anomalously high for the industry, in a newly-listed micro-cap with no institutional validation. That is precisely the profile the forensic modules are designed to filter out. The correct action is not to bet on the benign explanation — it is to **demand proof (cash) first.**
