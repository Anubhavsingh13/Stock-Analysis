## Executive Summary

Tirupati Forge is a Gujarat-based micro-cap precision forging exporter (₹162 Cr revenue, 90% exports) making a transformational bet on defence manufacturing — a new plant commissioned in March 2026 targets 155mm M107 artillery shell bodies with ₹250 Cr/year revenue aspiration. The thesis is credible in direction but completely unpriced in risk: at P/E 103×, the market has fully discounted successful defence execution at a time when the company has zero confirmed commercial defence revenue, zero disclosed order book from MoD/customers, and no track record in this segment. The existing operating business earns ~₹0.22–0.25 clean EPS (stripping non-operating treasury income that contributes 54% of Q4 PBT), implying a fair value of ₹12–18 for the current business. The ₹32+ premium at ₹50 is pure speculative option value on the defence plant. Weighted DCF expected value is ₹40 vs. current price ₹50.4 — 20% downside. The bear case (defence delays 2 years) implies ₹10–12 = 80% downside. Risk-reward is unfavourable at approximately 1:2.5. AVOID until confirmed defence orders and first revenue quarter are reported.

## Business Primer

**What the business does:**
Tirupati Forge Limited (NSE: TIRUPATIFL) is a Gujarat (Rajkot) based manufacturer of closed-die forged components and forged flanges, exporting approximately 90% of production to USA, Europe, Africa, Canada, and Malaysia. Two product families: (1) Auto & Industrial Components — auto parts, bearings, gears, crankshafts for automotive, railways, agriculture, and irrigation; and (2) Forged Flanges — carbon, stainless and specialty steel flanges for oil & gas pipelines and water infrastructure. Revenue is volume × realization on a 15,000 MT/annum installed capacity. FY26 revenue was ₹162 Cr. The company is now executing a new third vertical: 155mm M107 artillery shell bodies for global defence customers, with a new 150,000-unit/annum plant commissioned in March 2026. Commercial production targeted Q2 FY27. Management targets ₹250 Cr/year from this defence vertical — 1.5× the company's entire current revenue base. Phase 2 (doubling capacity to 300,000 units) is planned through a further FY27 equity raise.

**What kind of business this is and what to watch:**
Capital-intensive, asset-heavy precision metal manufacturing business — an export-oriented small-cap in a cyclical industry now executing a high-risk pivot into defence. The existing forging business earns thin margins (EBITDA 10–13%) typical of a commodity B2B exporter competing on quality and price. The ENTIRE stock re-rating from ₹30 to ₹50 (+67% in 52 weeks) is driven by one hypothesis: that the 155mm shell body plant will deliver ₹250 Cr in revenue and expand blended EBITDA margins toward 17–20%. Three structural risks define the analysis: (1) the defence plant has zero commercial revenue history — still in pre-commercial stage; (2) the operating business is barely profitable on a clean basis (non-operating treasury income = 54% of Q4 FY27 PBT); and (3) at P/E 103×, the market has discounted success before it has been demonstrated.
