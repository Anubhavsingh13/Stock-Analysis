## Business Primer
Garden Reach Shipbuilders & Engineers (GRSE), owned ~74.5% by the Government of India, operates three shipbuilding facilities in Kolkata specialising in warships — frigates, corvettes, fast patrol vessels, and survey/research ships for the Indian Navy and Coast Guard (89% of revenue, remainder from engineering products and deck machinery). Revenue flows on percentage-of-completion basis under MoD-allocated long-term contracts. GRSE's capital structure is unusual even by defence PSU standards: with only ₹584 Cr gross block (tangible PPE) against ₹7,002 Cr revenue, the company runs at an extraordinary 12× asset turnover — because MoD advance payments fund working capital, making GRSE essentially a government-financed assembly and engineering operation. The business absolutely depends on: (a) a steady pipeline of new MoD-allocated shipbuilding programmes and (b) maintaining the workforce skill and shipyard throughput capacity to execute at pace.

GRSE is a capital-light compounder within a government-controlled monopoly framework. Key value driver is execution velocity (ships delivered per year) and margin efficiency (fixed-price contracts with escalation). Key risk is programme transition gaps — the period between completion of one programme (P17A) and commencement of revenue from the next (P17B). GRSE's concentration in surface warships (vs. MDL's submarine advantage) means its revenue is more visible but also more cyclical with India's surface fleet build programme. The exceptional FY26 metrics (ROCE 43%, ROE 32%) should be interpreted as "peak cycle" benchmarks, not steady-state — sustainable ROIC is likely 20–25%.
