## Executive Summary

Viyash Scientific Ltd (formerly Sequent Scientific Ltd, renamed Jan 8, 2026) is an integrated pharmaceutical enterprise specialising in animal health APIs, formulations, human health, and CDMO services, operating across 100+ countries with 8+ manufacturing facilities. The company underwent a transformative NCLT-approved merger with Carlyle-owned Viyash Lifesciences in November 2025, nearly doubling its revenue base to ₹3,420 Cr in FY26 and vaulting EBITDA margins to a record 19.1% (Q4 FY26: 21.7%). The core business quality is genuinely improving — ICRA/Ind-Ra AA- rated, net debt near zero, and management targets 20%+ EBITDA margins by FY28 — but at a TTM PE of ~64x and EV/EBITDA of ~23x, the current price already prices in a near-perfect integration execution. The key risk is that standalone organic revenue CAGR was only ~5–6% pre-merger; if synergy capture disappoints, re-rating risk is severe at these multiples.

## Module 3 — Business Quality & Moat

### Greenwald's Three Barriers

**1. Supply/Cost Advantage:** ✅ PRESENT (Narrow)
- Vizag is India's only USFDA-approved dedicated veterinary API plant — structural, replication takes 5–7 years (FDA approval process + scale)
- 30+ commercial animal health APIs with global registrations — each registration took years to accumulate; collectively a strong portfolio barrier
- However, formulations segment is less protected; generic competition is real

**2. Customer Captivity:** ⚠️ PARTIAL
- Regulatory switching costs: once a vet API is sourced from a USFDA-approved facility, changing supplier requires costly re-registration (FDA DMF amendments, EMA notifications) → moderate captivity
- CDMO customers (8–10 innovators) face switching costs in contracted manufacture — high captivity
- Formulation distributors: lower captivity, can switch brands

**3. Local Economies of Scale:** ❌ ABSENT
- No single dominant geographic market; global presence but not dominant in any one region

**Buffett Moat Test (10% price increase without volume loss):**
- API business: Likely yes — regulatory approvals + unique Vizag positioning
- Formulations: Unlikely in commodity segments; possible in specialty/companion animal
- **Moat Verdict: Narrow** — API + CDMO has a real, defensible moat; formulations is competitive

**Moat Durability Tests:**
1. ROIC vs. WACC: FY26 ROCE 19% > WACC ~12.2% → spread positive, but historically volatile
2. Market share: Top 30 globally, stable in key markets; India gaining share
3. Pricing power: API segment demonstrates pricing discipline; pass-through of input costs seen in FY26
4. Activity system: API → Formulation → CDMO integration is synergistic and hard to replicate piecemeal
5. Competitor fear: Zoetis/Elanco use Viyash as an API supplier for some products → relationship moat
