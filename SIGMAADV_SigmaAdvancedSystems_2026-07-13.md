## Business Primer

Sigma Advanced Systems Ltd is a newly-assembled Aerospace & Defence (A&D) manufacturing platform created by reverse-merging privately-held Sigma Advanced Systems Pvt Ltd into the listed shell of the old Megasoft Ltd. It sells (or aspires to sell) precision aerospace components and defence systems — the reported portfolio spans avionics, naval/torpedo/submarine systems, communication and radar systems, counter-UAS (counter-drone), and precision-engineered aerospace parts. Customers are B2B/government: defence PSUs (India) plus Western aerospace OEMs (Rolls-Royce, Airbus, Boeing, GE Aerospace, Safran, Lockheed Martin) accessed largely through the acquired UK precision-engineering group **Nasmyth**. Revenue is project/order-book driven, with a dual India+UK manufacturing footprint. The business absolutely depends on two things it has not yet demonstrated at scale: (1) converting a claimed multi-thousand-crore order pipeline into executed, margin-positive revenue, and (2) integrating three cross-border acquisitions simultaneously while a <6-month-old listed entity.

This is best classified as a **transformation / narrative-arbitrage micro-to-midcap**, not a proven compounder. The key value driver the market is pricing is future order-book conversion (volume + mix); the key risks are execution/integration risk, earnings quality (headline profit is one-time asset-sale gains, not operations), and extreme valuation with a thin public float under exchange surveillance. The single structural feature colouring the entire analysis: **the growth is ~100% inorganic and the FY26 profit is ~100% non-operating** — so every headline ratio (ROE 90.6%, ROCE 60.8%, "172% profit CAGR") is an accounting artefact of the merger and asset monetisation, not a signal of business quality.
