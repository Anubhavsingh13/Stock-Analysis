## Business Primer

EPack Prefab Technologies (EPACK) is India's third-largest integrated Pre-Engineered Building (PEB) company, founded in 1999 and listed via IPO in January 2025 at ₹215/share. The company designs, manufactures, transports, and erects prefabricated steel structures on a turnkey basis — encompassing pre-engineered steel buildings (PEB), sandwich insulated panels, and light gauge steel framing. Revenue is generated on a project-contract basis: the key economic unit is tonnes of structural steel fabricated × realization per tonne, supplemented by sandwich panel output (priced per square metre). Approximately 85–90% of revenue comes from the PEB/Prefab division; the remaining 10–15% is the Expanded Polystyrene (EPS) packaging segment. Customers are exclusively B2B — industrial manufacturers, logistics warehousing players, data centre developers, cold chain operators, and government infrastructure projects. The company operates three manufacturing plants (Greater Noida, Ghiloth-Rajasthan, Mambattu-Andhra Pradesh) with combined structural steel capacity of 1,47,122 MTPA and sandwich panel capacity of 13.1 lakh sq m/year. The single most important earnings driver is order intake velocity × execution pace × steel price pass-through discipline.

EPACK is best classified as an asset-heavy project-manufacturing business with EPC characteristics — more capital-intensive and working-capital-hungry than a pure-play EPC but with more asset utilization leverage than a simple contractor. The key value driver is volume growth (capacity utilization) + mix improvement (data centres and cold chains pay premium over vanilla industrial sheds) + operating leverage as fixed manufacturing costs get absorbed over higher throughput. The central risk is steel price volatility (steel = 55–60% of input cost), lumpy revenue due to project-based execution (Q3 is structurally weak every year due to monsoon), and execution capability as the company rapidly expands from ₹245 Cr revenue (FY20) to a ₹2,000 Cr ambition (FY27). A structural feature: EPACK is still in early-to-mid ramp-up, with a ₹300 Cr IPO fresh issue (Jan 2025) funding aggressive capacity expansion, meaning FY26–FY28 is a capex-intensive period where FCF will be thin even as PAT grows.

## Module 1b — Sector Cycle: PEB / Prefabricated Construction

**Secular demand (embed in DCF):** PLI manufacturing build-out, National Logistics Policy warehousing hubs, data centre capacity addition (India: 4–5 GW by 2030 vs. <0.5 GW today), cold chain infrastructure (14–15% CAGR), China+1 shift. **Structural baseline: 12–15% CAGR for organised PEB market.**

**Cyclical demand (do NOT embed as permanent):** Government capex recovery post-election (H2 FY25/FY26), PLI deadline rush creating front-loaded private capex. This adds 15–20% to current demand; expected to moderate from FY28.

**Key insight:** EPACK's 30–35% revenue CAGR = 13–15% secular + 10–12% cyclical tailwind + 8–10% capacity expansion effect. Post-FY28 run-rate should settle at 15–18%.

**Competitive Capacity Risk:** Interarch (Gujarat new plant), Pennar (3L MTPA already), Zamil all expanding simultaneously. Oversupply risk from FY28–FY29 if demand normalises.

**Steel Price Pass-Through:** 80% of contracts renegotiated for steel price indexing (FY26). Company maintains 32,000-tonne buffer inventory.

**Sector Cycle Verdict: TAILWIND** — structural; 2–3 year visibility; cyclical deceleration risk from FY28.
