## Business Primer

SAR Televenture Limited (founded 2019, Noida) is an integrated telecom and digital infrastructure company registered as an Infrastructure Provider Category-I (IP-I) with the Department of Telecommunications. It earns revenue through four streams: (1) building, owning, and leasing 4G/5G telecom towers to operators like Airtel, Jio, BSNL, and Vodafone Idea; (2) deploying and managing Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH) broadband networks for housing societies, IT parks, and commercial complexes; (3) smart systems (security, surveillance, access management); and (4) enterprise connectivity solutions. In October 2025, the company acquired 100% stakes in Blue Lotus Support Services and Whitefield Communications for an aggregate consideration of ₹800 crore, adding approximately 4.5 lakh broadband subscribers in South India (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh). Revenue has scaled from ₹1 crore in FY21 to ₹522 crore in FY26 — a 5-year CAGR of 256% — though much of this growth is acquisition-driven.

The business is a hybrid of asset-heavy infrastructure (tower leasing — a scale-dependent model requiring 1.5–2.0× tenancy ratio to earn economic returns) and EPC-style project construction (tower deployment for telcos on a fee basis). It is not a consumer-facing business; all revenues are B2B. The critical value drivers are tower count, tenancy ratio per tower, FTTH home passes, and the ability to cross-sell smart systems to the same captive broadband base. The key structural risk is that SAR has only ~1,250 towers — a fraction of Indus Towers' 230,000+ — meaning it lacks the economies of scale that make tower leasing genuinely high-ROIC. The ₹800 crore acquisition, executed when the company's own market capitalisation was ₹715 crore, is the single most consequential and least-understood element of the investment thesis.
