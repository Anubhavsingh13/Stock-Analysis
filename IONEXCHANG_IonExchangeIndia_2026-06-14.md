## MODULE 1 — INDUSTRY STRUCTURE & CAPITAL CYCLE

### 1a. Porter Five Forces

| Force | Assessment | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **Supplier Power** | Moderate — raw materials (styrene, DVB, specialty chemicals) have few global suppliers; ION Exchange has domestic scale advantage | Medium |
| **Buyer Power** | Low-Medium — water treatment in power, pharma, and municipal sectors is regulated; switching costs high once designed into process | Medium-Low |
| **Threat of New Entrants** | Low — resin manufacturing requires significant capital, process know-how, and regulatory approvals (pharma/food-grade); Roha plant itself took 5+ years to build | Low |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | Low for resins (no cost-effective substitute for demineralisation in power/pharma); Medium for EPC (multiple competitors) | Medium |
| **Competitive Rivalry** | Medium — Thermax, VA Tech Wabag, L&T (water), SUEZ, Veolia compete on large projects; ION Exchange differentiated by end-to-end capability (resins + EPC + service) | Medium |

**Overall Industry Attractiveness: Moderate-High.** Regulated demand, high switching costs, and capital barriers protect the core chemicals/resins business. EPC is more commoditised.

### 1b. Sector Cycle

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Demand Drivers** | Structural: India's water infrastructure gap (₹7 lakh Cr AMRUT 2.0 + private capex); zero-liquid discharge mandates; pharma/semiconductor water purity requirements. Secular rate: ~14% CAGR |
| **Cyclical Overlay** | GCC (Gulf Cooperation Council) projects disrupted FY26 — Engineering segment revenue recognition deferred, not cancelled. Timing risk, not demand risk |
| **Input Costs** | Petrochemical derivatives (styrene) broadly stable in FY26; no material input cost headwind |
| **Competitive Capacity Pipeline** | No major resin manufacturing capacity announced by domestic competitors. ION Exchange's Roha plant creates a 3-5 year supply moat in domestic resins |
| **Inventory Cycle** | Neutral — customers ordering at consumption rate; destocking from FY25 pharma segment has normalised |

### 1c. Physical Capacity Model

**Chemicals / Resins Segment (AT model applies):**
- Roha plant total capacity: Ion exchange resins + specialty chemicals; designed for ~₹1,125 Cr revenue at full utilisation (2.5× AT on ₹450 Cr gross block)
- FY26 status: Cation stream stabilised; remaining streams commissioning; 25% utilisation target FY27
- Ramp type: **Brownfield-adjacent** (new plant on existing site with operational support)
  - Year 1: 25-30% utilisation | Year 2: 60-65% | Year 3: 85-90%
- Payback: 4-5 years per management guidance (implies steady-state NOPAT ~₹90-112 Cr from Roha alone)

**Engineering / EPC Segment (Order book model — NOT AT model):**
- Order book at FY26 end: ₹2,643 Cr (≈2 years of revenue coverage)
- FY26 order inflow: +40% YoY; Q3 FY26 quarterly inflow ₹516 Cr
- Legacy GCC project margin drag: ~1.5-2% EBIT dilution; expect resolution FY27-FY28
- Guided: "high single digit" EBIT margins on new orders vs current blended ~4%

### 10A-6. Incremental ROIC on Roha

```
Roha total capex: ₹450 Cr
```
